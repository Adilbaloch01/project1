Bedsheets are essential components of bedding, typically consisting of three main types: fitted sheets, flat sheets, and pillowcases. Fitted sheets are designed to cover the mattress snugly, while flat sheets serve as a top layer for comfort. Pillowcases are used to encase pillows.

            When it comes to bedsheet fabrics, various materials are 
            commonly used, including cotton, polyester, bamboo, and silk.
             Each fabric has its own characteristics, such as breathability, 
             softness, and durability. Regulations often focus on labeling, 
             flammability standards, and the use of chemicals in bedding 
             products Common Fabrics Used in Bedsheets.

            Cotton:
            
            Highly breathable and soft.
            Available in various weaves like percale and sateen.
            Durable and easy to care for.
            
            Polyester:
            Often blended with cotton for added durability.
            Wrinkle-resistant and quick-drying.
            Less breathable than natural fibers.
            
            Bamboo:
            Eco-friendly and biodegradable.
            Naturally hypoallergenic and moisture-wicking.
            Soft and silky feel.
            
            Silk:
            Luxurious and smooth texture.
            Naturally hypoallergenic but requires special care.
            Excellent temperature regulation.
            
            Percale:
            A plain-weave fabric known for its crisp feel.
            Typically has a thread count of 200 or higher.
            
            Sateen:
            A weave that gives a silky finish and sheen.
            Usually has a higher thread count, providing a soft feel.
            
            Jersey:
            Stretchy and soft, popular for casual bedding.
            Can snag easily, so care is needed.
